Orbis Galactic populates the galaxy with iconic Star Wars NPCs and creatures.

## Droids

### R2-D2 units

**R2-D2** (`OrbisGalactic_NPC_R2`)

Loyal astromech droids that can follow and assist you.

#### Characteristics

- **Type:** Generic NPC
- **Appearance model:** `OrbisGalactic_Model_R2_Blue`
- **Max health:** 50 HP
- **Invulnerable:** No
- **Default state:** Idle
- **Name translation:** `R2D2`

#### Movement

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Walk controller">
    - **Max walk speed:** 15
    - **Gravity:** 10
    - **Max fall speed:** 8
    - **Acceleration:** 10
    - **Hover frequency:** 0.2
    - **Max climb height:** 10
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Behavior">
    R2 units use sophisticated AI to follow players:
    
    - **Detection range:** 10 blocks
    - **Follow distance:** 3 blocks (stops at this distance)
    - **Teleport range:** Beyond 20 blocks
    - **Strafe behavior:** Maintains 2-3 block spacing from other droids
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

#### Interactions

<Info>
  You can interact with R2-D2 units. When you do, they drop their spawn egg and despawn, allowing you to pick them up and redeploy them elsewhere.
</Info>

**Drop on despawn:** R2D2 Droid Pod (`Egg_Spawner_R2`)

#### Variants

**R2-D2 (No Follower)** (`OrbisGalactic_NPC_R2_NoFollower`)

A stationary variant that doesn't follow players.

### Spawn eggs

**R2D2 Droid Pod** (`Egg_Spawner_R2D2`)

Deploy an R2-D2 unit by placing this item.

## Tatooine inhabitants

### Jawas

**Jawa** (`Jawa`)

Small scavengers native to Tatooine who trade in salvaged goods.

#### Characteristics

- **Type:** Variant of Kweebec Sapling template
- **Appearance model:** `Jawa_Model`
- **Max health:** 74 HP
- **Attitude group:** Humanoid_Friendly
- **Drop list:** `Drop_Jawa`
- **Is memory:** Yes (remembered by the world)
- **Name translation:** `server.npcRoles.Jawa.name`

<Tip>

## NPC organization

NPCs are organized using attitude groups and role systems:

<Accordion title="Attitude groups">
  **Humanoid_Friendly**
  
  Most non-hostile NPCs like Jawas belong to this group. They won't attack players on sight and can be interacted with for trading or quests.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Role templates">
  NPCs use role templates to define their behavior:
  
  - **Generic:** Basic NPC with standard AI
  - **Variant:** Modified version of an existing template (like Jawa from Kweebec Sapling)
  
  Templates define movement, combat, and interaction behaviors.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="NPC states">
  NPCs cycle through various states:
  
  - **Idle:** Default resting state
  - **Follow:** Actively following a player
  - **Combat:** Engaged in battle
  - **Despawn:** Removing from world
  
  State transitions are controlled by sensors and instructions.
</Accordion>

## NPC AI system

### Sensors

NPCs use sensors to detect and respond to their environment:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Player sensor">
    Detects nearby players within a specified range.
    
    **Example from R2-D2:**
    ```json
    "Sensor": {
      "Type": "Player",
      "Range": 10,
      "LockOnTarget": true
    }
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mob sensor">
    Detects other NPCs and creatures.
    
    Used by R2 units to maintain spacing from each other.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="State sensor">
    Monitors the NPC's current state (Idle, Despawn, etc.).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Target sensor">
    Checks if the NPC has an active target to follow or attack.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Motion controllers

NPCs use motion controllers to move through the world:

**Seek behavior**
- Approaches a target
- Slows down at `SlowDownDistance`
- Stops at `StopDistance`
- Uses `RelativeSpeed` multiplier

**Maintain distance behavior**
- Keeps a specific distance from target
- Strafes to avoid crowding
- Used when multiple NPCs follow the same player

**Teleport behavior**
- Instantly moves to target when too far
- Prevents NPCs from getting permanently lost
- R2 units teleport when beyond 20 blocks

## Drop lists

When NPCs are defeated or despawned, they drop items from configured drop lists:

- **Drop_Jawa:** Scrap metal and salvage items
- **Egg spawners:** Most companion NPCs drop their spawn egg when picked up

<Warning>
  Some hostile NPC variants will attack on sight. Be prepared for combat when encountering Jawa Sith or hostile Tusken Raiders!
</Warning>

## Spawning NPCs

You can spawn NPCs using spawn eggs:

1. Obtain the spawn egg from crafting, trading, or picking up an existing NPC
2. Right-click to place the NPC in the world
3. The NPC will spawn in its default idle state
4. Interact with it to set behaviors (follow, trade, etc.)

<Info>
  NPCs marked with `"IsMemory": true` are remembered by the world save system and will persist even when you're far away.
</Info>
